# VSI PDF Viewer — Mobile User Guide

A simple guide to viewing PDF documents on your phone or tablet.

---

## What You See

When you open the PDF Viewer on your phone, you will see:

    ┌─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┐
    │  ⏮ ◀  [ 1 ] / 5  ▶ ⏭ | − 100% + | Portrait | Single │  ← Toolbar
    ├─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┤
    │                                                     │
    │                                                     │
    │              Your PDF page                          │  ← PDF viewing area
    │              is shown here                          │
    │                                                     │
    │                                                     │
    └─────────────────────────────────────────────────────┘

The toolbar is at the top. The PDF page fills the rest of the screen.

On mobile, the toolbar is compact — only page navigation and zoom buttons are shown. The orientation and display mode labels are hidden to save space.

---

## The Toolbar

The toolbar has two groups of controls:

### Page navigation buttons

These let you move between pages:

    ⏮  ◀  [ 1 ] / 5  ▶  ⏭

| Button | What it does |
|---|---|
| ⏮ | Jump to the FIRST page |
| ◀ | Go to the PREVIOUS page |
| [ 1 ] / 5 | Shows you are on page 1 of 5. You can tap the number, type a different page number, and press Enter to jump there. |
| ▶ | Go to the NEXT page |
| ⏭ | Jump to the LAST page |

When you are already on the first page, the ⏮ and ◀ buttons turn gray and cannot be pressed. The same happens with ▶ and ⏭ on the last page.

### Zoom buttons

These let you make the page bigger or smaller:

    −  Zoom: 100%  +

| Button | What it does |
|---|---|
| − | Make the page SMALLER (zooms out by 25%) |
| Zoom: 100% | Shows the current zoom level. This updates automatically when you pinch to zoom. |
| + | Make the page BIGGER (zooms in by 25%) |

### Protection indicator

If the document is protected, you will also see:

    🔒 Protected

This means downloading and/or printing has been disabled. See the "Protected Documents" section below.

## Scrolling

When the PDF page is larger than your screen (for example after zooming in), you can scroll to see the rest of the page.

**How to scroll:**

1. Place ONE finger on the PDF
2. Drag your finger up, down, left, or right
3. The page moves in the direction you drag

This works just like scrolling a website or a photo.

    ┌──────────────┐
    │              │
    │    ☝️ drag    │  ← One finger, drag in any direction
    │    ↕️ ↔️      │
    │              │
    └──────────────┘

## Pinch to Zoom

You can zoom in to see small text or details, and zoom out to see the full page.

**How to zoom in:**

1. Place TWO fingers on the PDF
2. Spread your fingers APART (move them away from each other)
3. The page gets bigger

**How to zoom out:**

1. Place TWO fingers on the PDF
2. Pinch your fingers TOGETHER (move them toward each other)
3. The page gets smaller

    Zoom in:                Zoom out:

    ┌──────────────┐       ┌──────────────┐
    │              │       │              │
    │   👆  ←→  👆  │       │   👆  →←  👆  │
    │   spread     │       │   pinch      │
    │   apart      │       │   together   │
    └──────────────┘       └──────────────┘

**Tips:**

- The spot between your two fingers stays in place while you zoom. So put your fingers on the part you want to look at. This works even when you are scrolled to the middle of a large page — the zoom always anchors to the content under your fingers.
- While you zoom, the page may look slightly blurry. When you lift your fingers, it sharpens up. This is normal — the viewer shows a fast preview during the gesture and renders a sharp image when you let go.
- There is a minimum and maximum zoom level. The default range is 50% to 400%, but this can be configured. The zoom percentage in the toolbar always shows the actual current level.

## Double-Tap to Zoom

A quick way to zoom in and out without using two fingers.

**How to zoom in with a double-tap:**

1. Tap the spot you want to look at TWICE quickly (like a double-click)
2. The page zooms in to 2.5x around that spot

**How to zoom back out:**

1. Double-tap anywhere on the page again
2. The page zooms back to the normal fit-to-width view

    Double-tap to zoom in:       Double-tap to zoom out:

    ┌──────────────┐            ┌──────────────┐
    │              │            │ ████████████ │
    │    👆👆       │            │ ████████████ │
    │    tap tap   │     →      │ ██ 👆👆 █████ │    →    back to normal
    │              │            │ ████████████ │
    └──────────────┘            └──────────────┘
    normal view                  zoomed in

**Tips:**

- Both taps must be close together (within about 1 cm) and fast (within 0.3 seconds)
- If you tap too slowly or move your finger between taps, it will NOT zoom

## Swiping Between Pages

When you are in single-page mode, you can swipe to go to the next or previous page.

### When the page fits on screen (not zoomed in)

This is the simplest case. Just swipe horizontally:

**Swipe left to go to the NEXT page:**

1. Place one finger on the PDF
2. Swipe quickly to the LEFT
3. You move to the next page

**Swipe right to go to the PREVIOUS page:**

1. Place one finger on the PDF
2. Swipe quickly to the RIGHT
3. You move to the previous page

    Next page:              Previous page:

    ┌──────────────┐       ┌──────────────┐
    │              │       │              │
    │    👆 ←←←    │       │    →→→ 👆    │
    │    swipe     │       │       swipe  │
    │    left      │       │       right  │
    └──────────────┘       └──────────────┘

### When the page is zoomed in (page is bigger than the screen)

When you are zoomed in, swiping normally scrolls (pans) the page. Page navigation only happens when you have scrolled all the way to the edge:

**To go to the NEXT page:**

1. First, scroll all the way to the RIGHT edge of the page
2. Once you are at the right edge, swipe LEFT quickly
3. You move to the next page

**To go to the PREVIOUS page:**

1. First, scroll all the way to the LEFT edge of the page
2. Once you are at the left edge, swipe RIGHT quickly
3. You move to the previous page

    Step 1: Scroll to edge       Step 2: Swipe at edge

    ┌──────────────┐            ┌──────────────┐
    │         edge│|            │         edge│|
    │    👆 ←←←   │|            │    👆 ←←←   │|
    │    scroll to│|    then    │    swipe to  │|  → next page!
    │    the edge │|            │    navigate  │|
    └──────────────┘            └──────────────┘

**Important:** You must ALREADY be at the edge BEFORE you start swiping. If you scroll to the edge and keep going in the same motion, it will NOT change pages. This prevents accidental page turns.

**Tips for a successful swipe:**

- The swipe must be fast (less than 0.4 seconds)
- The swipe must be mostly horizontal (not too much up or down movement)
- The swipe must travel at least about 1.5 cm (60 pixels)
- You must already be at the edge when the swipe starts

## When the Page is Taller Than the Screen

Sometimes the PDF page is taller than your phone screen, even at normal zoom. This happens with long pages or when zoomed in.

**To see the rest of the page:**

1. Place one finger on the PDF
2. Drag UP to see more of the bottom of the page
3. Drag DOWN to see more of the top of the page

This is just normal scrolling and works at any zoom level.

## Jump to a Specific Page

If you want to go directly to page 7 (or any other page):

1. Tap the page number in the toolbar (the number between ◀ and ▶)
2. The number becomes editable — a keyboard appears
3. Clear the current number and type the page you want (e.g. 7)
4. Press Enter / Go on your keyboard
5. The viewer jumps to that page

    ┌─────────────────────────────────────┐
    │  ⏮ ◀  [ 7 ] / 12  ▶ ⏭ | − 85% + │  ← Tap the "7" to edit
    ├─────────────────────────────────────┤

## Protected Documents

Some PDFs may be protected by the person who set up the viewer. When a document is protected, you will see a lock icon in the toolbar:

    ⏮ ◀  [ 1 ] / 5  ▶ ⏭  |  − 100% +  |  🔒 Protected

**What "Protected" means:**

- You CAN still view the PDF normally
- You CAN still zoom, scroll, swipe, and navigate pages
- You CAN still search for text
- You CANNOT download or save the PDF
- You CANNOT print the PDF
- Long-pressing will NOT show a "Save" or "Share" menu
- Right-clicking (on tablets with mouse support) will NOT show a context menu

The tooltip on the lock icon tells you exactly what is disabled (download, printing, or both).

---

## Search for Text

On mobile, a compact search bar appears above the PDF viewer.

**How to search:**

1. Tap the search box at the top
2. Type the word or phrase you are looking for
3. Tap the 🔎 button (or press Enter on your keyboard)
4. All matching words are highlighted in yellow on the PDF
5. The view scrolls to the first match

**How to clear the search:**

1. Tap the ✕ button next to the search box
2. All yellow highlights disappear

**Tips:**

- The search ignores uppercase/lowercase: searching for "hello" also finds "Hello" and "HELLO"
- The search ignores accents: searching for "curacao" also finds "Curaçao"

## Desktop Features

When using the viewer on a desktop computer with a mouse and keyboard, you have additional controls:

### Mouse

| Action | Result |
|---|---|
| Click and drag | Pan / scroll the PDF (cursor changes to a hand) |
| Ctrl + mouse wheel | Zoom in / out centered on the cursor position |
| Fast horizontal drag at scroll edge | Navigate to next / previous page |

### Keyboard

| Key | Action |
|---|---|
| ← → | Scroll horizontally, or navigate pages when content fits |
| ↑ ↓ | Scroll vertically |
| Ctrl + ← | Go to previous page (instant) |
| Ctrl + → | Go to next page (instant) |

When using arrow keys at the edge of a page, the first press is absorbed (does nothing). The second press navigates to the next/previous page. This prevents accidental page turns while scrolling.

### Desktop toolbar extras

On desktop (768px and wider), the toolbar also shows:

- Current orientation (Portrait / Landscape)
- Current display mode (Single / Continuous)

The demo page provides additional desktop-only controls: file upload, zoom presets (25%–1000%), orientation selector, display mode selector, print buttons, download buttons, and protection toggles.

## Troubleshooting

| Problem | Solution |
|---|---|
| Swiping does not change pages | Make sure you are at the edge of the page first. Swipe faster and more horizontally. |
| Double-tap does not zoom | Tap faster (both taps within 0.3 seconds) and keep your finger in the same spot. |
| Page looks blurry while pinching | This is normal. The page sharpens when you lift your fingers. |
| The whole browser zooms instead of the PDF | This should not happen. If it does, refresh the page. |
| Toolbar buttons are gray | Gray buttons mean you cannot go further in that direction (e.g. you are on the first page so "previous" is disabled). |
| Cannot download or print | The document is protected. Look for the 🔒 icon in the toolbar. |
| Page says "Loading PDF..." | Wait a moment. The PDF library is being downloaded for the first time. After the first load, it is cached by your browser. |
| Zoom percentage does not match the preset I selected | Pinch or Ctrl+wheel zoom overrides the preset. The toolbar always shows the actual effective zoom. Tap − or + to adjust from the current level. |
| Arrow keys do not work | Click once on the PDF area to give it focus. The viewer should auto-focus on load, but clicking a toolbar button can steal focus. |
